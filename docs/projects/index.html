Projects

Hi! Welcome to my projects page. It’s a little eclectic.

For most of my career I worked as a computational cognitive scientist, so there’s quite a lot of academic research projects listed on this page. These days I work as a data scientist and unsurprisingly I have data science projects and software projects. Just to make life complicated, I’ve also listed some art and community projects that I’ve been involved in over the years. They make me happy.

Data Science

I write a data science blog, Notes from a data witch: posts cover a variety of topics including R, generative art, and Apache Arrow. I’ve also put together a collection of slide decks that I’ve used when teaching data science concepts, as well as a series of videos on YouTube where I talk about these topics.

Academic

It touched on topics in cognition, machine learning, statistics, visual perception, data visualisation, language, cultural evolution, cognitive development, and forensic psychology. Software

I have several R packages and other software at varying stages of development:

  • Some tools aid behavioural scientists: the jaysire package provides a method for writing behavioural experiments within R that can run through a web browser, and the lsr package accompanies my introductory statistics textbook
  • Some packages are useful in literate programming. I use bs4cards to create the thumbnail images on this site. The site itself is built using the simple calade hugo theme I wrote, and my art website is an example of a diziet themed site
  • Some packages exist as a small source of joy: the rainbowr package provides a way to generate LGBTIQ themed R hex stickers
  • Finally, there are the art packages: asciify, jasmines, scrawl, flametree, turmite59, and so on. These are mostly for my own joy, but I also use them in my R classes to help students to learn programming concepts

Community

  • In 2018 I co-founded R-Ladies Sydney with Jen Richmond, Lisa Williams, and Steph Stammel. R-Ladies is a worldwide organisation that promotes gender diversity within the R community and provides support across the entire spectrum of roles whether as leaders, package developers, conference speakers, conference participants, educators, or users.

  • During my academic career I held editorial positions at one of the top scientific journals (Science) and one of the top journals in psychology (Psychological Review). I also served as an associate editor at three top journals in cognitive science and cognitive psychology: Cognitive Science, Behavior Research Methods, and Open Mind.
